# Split Vote

## Definition

A voting system in which two or more candidates start simultaneously, potentially leading to an indefinite outcome and a new term beginning.

## Key Principles

* The election may fail if no candidate receives the necessary majority.
* In such cases, a new term begins, and the process is repeated until a winner emerges.

## Examples

* A classic example of split vote occurs in a scenario where two candidates are tied for the most votes, requiring a runoff election to break the tie.
* Another instance might be when multiple candidates have similar support levels, making it difficult to declare a clear winner.
